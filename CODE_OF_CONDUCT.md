# Code of Conduct

This policy applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, the security
advisory flow, and any other project-related interaction around
`latex2ufdissertation`.

## Expected behaviour

- Disagree respectfully; critique ideas, not people.
- Offer constructive, specific feedback over vague dismissal.
- English is the working language; be patient with non-native speakers.
- Argue on technical merit, not status or affiliation.
- Attribute prior work, ideas, and code accurately.
- Assume good faith from contributors and reporters.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, personal attacks, or insults directed at participants.
- Publishing others' private information (doxxing) without consent.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome advances.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or issue threads.
- Spam, off-topic marketing, or promotional brigading.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a conduct concern in good faith.

## Scope

Applies across all GitHub repository surfaces (issues, pull requests,
discussions, security advisories, releases) and any project-affiliated
channels operated by the maintainer.

## Reporting

Email `hugh.stats@gmail.com` with subject prefix `[latex2ufdissertation conduct]`.
The maintainer is the sole enforcer (solo project) and aims to respond
within 7 days. For security vulnerabilities, do not use this channel —
see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead.

## Enforcement

Responses are graduated and proportional to severity and recurrence:

1. Private warning with explanation.
2. Public warning on the affected thread.
3. Temporary block from project surfaces.
4. Permanent ban from the project.
